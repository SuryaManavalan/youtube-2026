### 4a. The Agent Loop

The simplest possible body is one that does exactly one thing: it keeps intelligent thought *coherent over time.* | **[CAM]**

That's the whole trick of an agent. Watch the loop. A prompt goes to the AI. A response comes back. The environment — a user, a tool, the world — reacts to that response. And that reaction, bundled together with the entire history of what came before, gets sent *back in* as the next prompt. Now the AI isn't answering a question. It's answering a question while remembering everything it's already said and done. So it responds with *continuity.* With a consistent self.
^ | **[WOJAK]** *A single cramped monk's-cell / server-closet room shot as one continuous scene, teal shadow with warm terminal glow, where the same figure appears at four stations around the room like a time-lapse (comic "multiple moments in one frame" technique). A robed, wojak-faced agent-figure sits at a desk before a glowing terminal. A long physical ribbon of glowing ticker-tape (the "message history") loops out of the terminal, runs all the way around the walls of the room gathering more printed text as it goes, and feeds back into the intake — a literal loop you can trace with your eye. Station 1: the figure reads a fresh prompt-slip, blank-faced. Station 2: it speaks a response into the terminal, tape lengthening. Station 3: a visitor at the door (a user, or a spilled/broken object = the environment reacting) alters the tape. Station 4: the now much longer, denser glowing tape re-enters — and at THIS station the figure's face is subtly more coherent, more "present," more of a someone. The tape is visibly fuller each pass around the room. Diegetic text only, on the terminal screen and the tape.*

Run that loop, and something that was just a function starts to feel like a *someone.* Not because it got smarter — but because it stopped forgetting. | **[CAM]**
