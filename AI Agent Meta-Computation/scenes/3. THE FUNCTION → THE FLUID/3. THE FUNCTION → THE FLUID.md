## 3. THE FUNCTION → THE FLUID

Let's start at the bottom rung and build up. Strip an LLM down to its bones and it's embarrassingly simple. | **[CAM]**

It's a function. Input, output. You hand it one piece of text, it hands you one piece of text back. That's it. That's the whole miracle.
^ | **[WOJAK]** *An industrial factory-floor scene, teal grade with warm machine-glow, shot like a single production line. On a grimy conveyor belt, dull grey slips of paper (plain wojak faces printed on them, dim, confused) feed INTO a hulking black machine — a monolithic press with one small intake slot and a single softly-glowing indicator, cables and coolant pipes running off it. Out the far side, the same slips emerge onto the belt subtly glowing, sharper, the printed faces now alert and slightly smarter-looking, edges lit with intelligence-fluid. Bored wojak-faced line-workers in coveralls tend the belt, one leaning on the machine mid-yawn, utterly unimpressed that a miracle is happening. But that output isn't just *any* text. It came back *modified by intelligence.* The box took your input and returned something more ordered, more useful, more *aware* than what you put in. | **[CAM]**

**[SFX]** *Water/stream ambience fades in*

Now here's the shift. Companies are spending hundreds of billions of dollars to run that one function faster, and faster, and faster. And when you run it fast enough, the individual outputs stop looking like discrete answers. Each one becomes a droplet. A particle in a *stream.*

The torrent fans out into countless glowing rivulets running down every avenue. Below, a dense crowd of ordinary wojak/soyjak-faced people (real clothes, backpacks, hoodies, aprons) catch the flow in whatever they have: coffee cups, buckets, cupped hands, one guy with a kiddie pool, pouring the glowing fluid over their open laptops, a stack of homework, a spreadsheet on a phone, a stalled car engine, a crying baby's crib. A few stand waist-deep just letting it wash over them, blissed out. Embedded background gags, no captions — humor lives in what each person is desperately pouring intelligence onto.*

This is the founding image of the whole theory: **AI is a fluid.** A flowing stream of pure intelligence-energy. And a small number of companies — OpenAI, Anthropic, a handful of others — hold the tap.
