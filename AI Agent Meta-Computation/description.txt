We picture AI as a brain. Glowing neurons, sparks, the usual render. That picture is useless, because nobody actually interacts with a synapse.

This is an attempt at a better picture. A way of seeing agentic AI as its own layer of reality, something I am calling metacomputation. Intelligence behaves like a fluid. Agents are the bodies that capture part of that flow. And the frameworks we build to give those bodies a mind start to look, unsettlingly, like the oldest maps of the soul.

None of this is peer reviewed or institutional. It is a personal interpretation. A lens. If it is useful, keep it. If it is not, cry me a river.
