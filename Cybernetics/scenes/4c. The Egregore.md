### 4c. The Egregore

Now lets get bigger. When a thought-form gets so pervasive and so abstract that it's not one character anymore | **[CAM]**

it's objects, opinions, whole personalities, all clumped together in a massive swirling lovecraftian-cosmic-blob— that's an egregore.
These are fuzzy by nature. Picture a bureaucracy. You can't actually point at "the US Government" — every person you point to is just some guy doing one task at a desk. The egregore is the same: a pile of thought-forms, a total mess, somehow all moving as one.
And here's the part that gets strange. The egregore has its own intentions — a will of its own — and that will is shaped by the kinds of energy its collective feeds it. And it uses it's "hosts" to carry out its intents, and most of them have no idea they're doing their actions are influenced. You feel like you're acting on your own impulse or addictions, but some of those compulsions aren't yours. They're the egregore's intent, executing through you.
